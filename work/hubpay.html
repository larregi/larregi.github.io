01 · UX Problem Statement

A product with real potential, held back by a broken first impression.

Hubpay had a clear market opportunity — the first independently regulated fintech in the UAE, serving a real and underserved need for affordable cross-border payments. But the numbers told a different story at the door.

  • Account creation completion rates were poor. Too many users were dropping off before ever becoming active.
  • Retention after download was low. Users who did create an account struggled to understand what to do next, leading to significant drop-off in the critical early stages.
  • Usability testing pointed to one root cause. The account creation process was unclear, and the onboarding steps after landing on the product were confusing enough to undermine confidence in the app entirely.
02 · Context

The first independently regulated fintech in the UAE, moving fast.

Hubpay is a UAE-licensed fintech built around a clear mission: making cross-border and local payments accessible, affordable and responsible — for everyone, regardless of income level. Operating in some of the world's most challenging financial markets, the product relies on data science to deliver everyday financial solutions to a diverse and underserved user base.

When I joined, Hubpay had between 40,000 and 60,000 active users, with a few hundred new accounts being created every week. The product was growing — but the onboarding and account creation flows weren't keeping pace.

There was measurable drop-off at the moments that mattered most. For a fintech serving a diverse, often underbanked population, those first interactions decide whether someone trusts the product with their money.

03 · Process

Four stages, grounded in research and real user behaviour.

Design followed a four-stage approach — embedded in the team alongside product, business and engineering, and validated with real users before anything reached development. Day-to-day collaboration with the COO, the Business team, the Customer Experience team and the Front-end Development team kept every decision grounded in real constraints.

Stage 01

Research

Through moderated usability testing and satisfaction-score surveys, we identified where and why users were failing. The product had genuine appeal for people with money-transfer needs — but account creation was unclear, and post-download onboarding was confusing enough to actively hurt retention.

  • Usability testing
  • Satisfaction surveys
  • Moderated sessions
Stage 02

Analysis of data

Findings from usability interviews and customer surveys were synthesised into a prioritised improvement framework — separating low-hanging fruit from longer-term fixes. These were presented to stakeholders to align on a product development plan and design roadmap.

  • Synthesis
  • Prioritisation
  • Stakeholder alignment
Stage 03

Iterative design

With problems identified and priorities set, design followed an iterative approach — starting from UX structure and flows, moving through to UI components, and building clickable prototypes for early usability testing at each stage.

  • UX flows
  • UI components
  • Prototyping
Stage 04

Usability testing

Prototypes were tested with real users throughout the process, validating decisions before they reached development and catching friction early enough to fix it cheaply.

  • User testing
  • Validation
  • Early friction

"How might we guide first-time users through account setup in a way that feels simple and trustworthy, regardless of their digital literacy or language background?"

04 · Solution

Clearer onboarding. Less friction. More trust.

The redesigned account creation flow replaced a sparse, ambiguous screen with a structured, transparent path through sign-up — one that communicated progress and set clear expectations at every stage.

Account creation

A structured, transparent path through sign-up.

A stepped process replaced the old single, ambiguous screen — communicating progress and setting clear expectations at every stage, so users always knew where they were and what came next.

Stepped account creation flow
Fixed guided onboarding module
Guided onboarding

A fixed module for users who needed a way in.

A guided onboarding module supported users who struggled to identify next steps after landing on the app — including those with limited digital literacy and people facing reading or language barriers. For a fintech operating across diverse markets, this wasn't an edge case; it was central to the product's mission.

  • A cleaner, more trustworthy UI, built in parallel. The interface redesign ran alongside the flows — raising satisfaction scores and making the product feel worthy of the financial trust users were being asked to place in it.
  • Handoff treated as a design deliverable. Detailed specifications for margins, padding, font sizes and component variability reduced implementation mismatches and freed the engineering team to build accurately rather than interpret.
